Kaju katli (literally "cashew slice"), also known as kaju barfi, is an Indian dessert. Kaju means cashew; barfi is often made by thickening milk with sugar and other ingredients (such as dry fruits and mild spices). Kesar kaju katli includes saffron.


Kaju Katli is a popular Indian sweet that is made with cashews and sugar. It is believed to have originated in the state of Gujarat, in western India. This is the most straight forward Indian sweet. Ground cashews with sugar syrup.
